Cowboys vs. Seahawks line, odds and predictions: Our experts like Dallas to beat Seattle

Publish date: 2024-05-30

The Athletic has live coverage of the Dallas Cowboys vs. Seattle Seahawks on Thursday Night Football

The Dallas Cowboys look to extend their 13-game home winning streak Thursday against the visiting Seattle Seahawks. Both teams are coming off divisional games played on Thanksgiving, with the Cowboys dominating the Commanders and the Seahawks getting dominated by the 49ers. Coming into the game, the Cowboys are comfortably in the playoffs, but the Seahawks are fighting for their playoff lives. Seattle has won four straight matchups with Dallas. Dallas is a big favorite.

Advertisement

Kickoff is set for 8:15 p.m. ET on Thursday at AT&T Stadium. The game will be televised on Amazon Prime.

The Cowboys are one of the hottest teams in the NFL and dominant on both sides of the ball. They lead the league in scoring (31.5) and give up the fourth-fewest points per game (16.8). Dak Prescott has played himself into the MVP conversation. Over the past five games, Prescott has thrown for more than 300 yards in four of five contests and has a touchdown-to-interception ratio of 17:2. He also chipped in a rushing touchdown. Tony Pollard comes into the game with touchdowns in two straight after not scoring since Week 1. He’ll look to continue his resurgence against a Seahawks defense that’s been susceptible to the run. Seattle has given up 14 rushing touchdowns and allows an average of 117.7 rushing yards per game.

Geno Smith failed to duplicate last season’s magic, which led to being named 2022 Comeback Player of the Year. Dallas has kept all their opponents under 20 points during their three-game winning streak. Injuries have certainly played a role in Seattle’s offensive struggles. Smith is dealing with an elbow injury, DK Metcalf has battled through hip and rib injuries, and Kenneth Walker is battling an oblique injury. Walker missed Week 12 and his status for Week 13 is doubtful.
