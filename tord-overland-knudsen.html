Biography

Tord Overland Knudsen was born in Norway on July 24, 1982. Famous as a bassist, keyboard player, and backup singer for the British indie band The Wombats, he contributed to the such UK hit albums as A Guide to Love, Loss & Desperation; The Modern Glitch; and Glitterbug. He and his fellow The Wombats members were featured on comedian Adam Hills‘ music trivia program Spicks and Specks. He and his bandmates launched The Wombats while studying at The Liverpool Institute for Performing Arts. Their early radio hits include “Moving to New York” and “Let’s Dance to Joy Division.”
