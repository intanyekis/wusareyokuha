American comedic actor Ed Helms is probably best known for his role as Andy Bernard in the hit series The Office, but he also made number of roles in various movies. The massive breakthrough came to Helm’s career when he attended an open audition for the show The Daily Show in 2002. He booked the role of a correspondent and spent the next four years just performing on some of its various segments.

Over the course of Ed Helm’s career, he has been known to play numerous roles in comedy movies. Although he has begun his journey in the show business as a standup comedian and as a voice over artist, he eventually gained some widespread notoriety as a film actor. Listed below are the top ten highest grossing movies of Ed Helms.

Ron Gone Wrong

With over $23,009,270 domestic gross and $57,339,313 worldwide box office, Ron’s Gone Wrong released on October 22, 2021 lands on the tenth spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by Locksmith Animation is about a socially awkward schoolboy named Barney who received a robot named Ron. The robot can walk, talk and is a digitally connected device that will act as Barney’s best friend. Barney is excited to finally have a robot of his own until his new toy starts to malfunction which draws the attention of an executive from the manufacturing company.

Tag

With over $54,730,625 domestic gross and $77,024,944 worldwide box office, Tag released on June 15, 2018 lands on the ninth spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by New Line Cinema is about the five highly competitive friends who hit the ground running for a no holds barred game of tag. The five friends are risking their jobs, relationships and necks in order to take down one another.

Vacation

With over $58,884,188 domestic gross and $102,306,500 worldwide box office, Vacation released on July 29, 2015 lands on the eight spot of the list. The comedy adventure movie produced by New Line Cinema is about Rustry Griswold who surprises his wife and their two sons to a trip to the Walley World. The cross country trip to America’s favorite fun park will make the family bond and have some much needed family bonding.

Captain Underpants: The First Epic Movie

With over $73,921,000 domestic gross and $126,422,949 worldwide box office, Captain Underpants: The First Epic Movie released on June 2, 2017 lands on the seventh spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by DreamWorks Animation is about George Beard and Harold Hutchins who are both overly imaginative pranksters who will spend hours on their tree house to create comic books.

Evan Almighty

With over $100,289,690 domestic gross and $174,131,329 worldwide box office, Evan Almighty released on June 22, 2007 takes the sixth spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by Spyglass Entertainment is about the newscaster Evan Baxter who left Buffalo when he won a seat in Congress. Evan moved his entire family to the Northern Virginia where he seems to have made it. However God threw a crisis at their feet where they tried everything to resolve the issue.

We’re the Millers

With over $150,394,119 domestic gross and $267,816,276 worldwide box office, We’re the Millers released on August 7, 2013 takes the fifth spot of the list. The movie produced by New Line Cinema is about a small time pot dealer, David, who learned the hard way that no good deed will ever go unpunished. He was trying to help some teens when he was mugged by thugs which made him lose all his money and stash. Now that David is in huge debt to his pot supplier, he will have to go to Mexico to pick up the latest shipment for his supplier.

Dr. Seuss’ The Lorax

With over $214,030,500 domestic gross and $350,976,753 worldwide box office, Dr. Seuss’ The Lorax released on March 2, 2012 lands on the fourth spot of the list. The adventure movie produced by Illumination Entertainment is about a twenty year old guy named Ted who lives in a place that is virtually devoid of nature where there are no flowers and tree growing. Ted is determined to win the heart of Audrey but in order to do this, he must find something that she truly desires, a Truffula tree.

The Hangover 3

With over $112,200,072 domestic gross and $362,000,072 worldwide box office, The Hangover 3 released on May 23, 2013 lands on the third spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by Legendary Pictures is about the gang known as the Wolfpack who narrowly escaped a disaster in Bangkok. Upon their escape, Phil, Stu and Doug currently have a happy life while Alan does not. Alan gone off his meds and has surrendered to his natural impulses which made the Wolfpack gang take on a journey to get him through some personal crisis.

The Hangover

With over $277,322,503 domestic gross and $465,487,583 worldwide box office, The Hangover released on June 5, 2009 takes the second spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by Legendary Pictures is about the memorable stag party of Doug and his three friends just two days before Doug’s wedding. The three groomsmen woke up the next morning without a memory of the what happened the night before and with Doug missing. With very little time to spare, the three re-trace their steps to find Doug and find out what happened the night before.

The Hangover Part II

With over $254,464,305 domestic gross and $586,764,305 worldwide box office, The Hangover Part II released on May 26, 2011 takes on the first spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by Legendary Pictures is about Stu’s turn to get married in Thailand since his bride is from Thailand. With desperation to avoid the same fate that happened to Doug’s stag party, Stu chose not to have alcohol at his pre- wedding events.
