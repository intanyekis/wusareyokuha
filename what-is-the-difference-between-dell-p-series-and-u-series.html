What is the Difference Between Dell P Series and U Series

Publish date: 2024-05-31

Over time technology has created many gadgets and tools for reducing manpower and consuming less time. One of them is computers, which are mostly accessible and affordable for people to work on. The size and quality of the monitors are necessary to be considered because that helps in increasing the productivity of the work. Dell is one of the companies that manufacture computers, laptops, notebooks.

Dell P Series vs Dell U Series

The main difference between Dell P Series and Dell U Series is that the display of the screen panel for the P series is dull when if compared, on the other hand, with the Dell U Series. The maximum brightness shown by the Dell P series is around 250 cd/m2, whereas comparatively, on the other hand, the maximum brightness shown by the Dell U Series is about 300 cd/m2.

Dell P Series monitors of the Dell company stands for the Professional series of monitors. The screen size of the P Series monitors is about 23.8 inches, and it has an IPS panel type. The weight of the P series monitors is around 7.19 lbs which are quite light. The screen display of the P Series monitors is coated by Antiglare with 3H.

Dell U Series monitors that are manufactured by the Dell company stand for the UltraSharp series of monitors. The screen size of the U Series monitors is about 24 inches and also has an IPS panel type. The UltraSharp series monitors have a very bright screen display and can be increased up to 300 cd/m2. The screen display is coated by the Antiglare with 3H. 

Comparison Table Between Dell P Series and U Series

What is Dell P Series?

Dell P Series of monitors are the monitors that stand for the Professional series and are being manufactured under it. The monitors are created with such ease that there is distraction-free viewing. The exterior design shows that it is developed for the workstations and has ultra-thin bezels that increase the display area on its three sides.

The size of the screen of the monitors is around 23.8 inches and has a dull brightness compared to the U series. The brightness of the monitors can be maximized up to 250 cd/m2. The backside of the monitor panel is the place where different connecting ports are placed. These ports are slightly above the bottom edge of the panel, and they are – USB 2.0, 3.0, CGA, HDMI, and DisplayPort.

The display screen of monitors is coated with Antiglare and 3H (full high definition). The native resolution of the screen is around 1900 × 1080 pixels, and they eventually weigh approximately 19 lbs, including packaging.  

What is Dell U Series?

Dell U Series monitors are the monitors that stand for the UltraSharp series and are being manufactured under it. The external design of the Dell U Series moreover resembles with the P Series and has ultra-thin bezels on the three side that increases the display area while along with, it provides distraction-free viewing. These are also developed for the workstations and appear very professional.

The size of the screen for the U Series is about 24 inches. The monitor has luminescent brightness and can be maximized up to 300 cd/m2. The rear-view panel or the backside of the monitor is the place where the different connecting ports are present. These connecting ports are near to the bottom edge of the panel and are – USB 3.0, HDMI, Audio Lineout, Display Port, and mini-DisplayPort.

The screen display of the monitor has an Antiglare coating with 3H along with a native resolution of about 1900 * 1200 pixels. The monitors of U Series approximately weigh around 7 lb with packaging.

Main Differences Between Dell P Series and U Series

  • The Dell P Series monitors stand for the monitors of the Professional series, whereas comparatively, on the other hand, the monitors manufactured under the Dell U Series are the monitors that stand for the UltraSharp series. 
  • The size of the screen of the Dell P Series is around 23.8 inches in dimensions, whereas comparatively, on the other hand, the size of the screen of the Dell U Series is around 24 inches in dimensions. 
  • The native resolution of the Dell P Series is about 1920 × 1080 pixels, whereas comparatively, on the other hand, the native resolution of the Dell U Series is about 1920 × 1200 pixels. 
  • Dell P Series monitors have a somewhat dull monitor appearance, and it can be maximized up to 250 cd/m2 whereas comparatively, on the other hand, the Dell U Series monitors have illuminating monitor appearance that can be maximized up to 300 cd/m2.
  • The monitor of Dell P Series comes with five different ports, and that is – USB 2.0, 3.0, CGA, HDMI, and DisplayPort whereas comparatively, on the other hand, the monitor of Dell U Series also comes with five different ports, and that is – USB 3.0, HDMI, Audio Lineout, Display Port, and mini-DisplayPort.
  • The weight of the monitors with the packaging of Dell P Series is around 19.80 lb, whereas comparatively, on the other hand, the weight of the monitors with the packaging of Dell U Series is around 7.19 lb.
  • The price of the Dell P Series monitors is less whereas comparatively, on the other hand, the price of the Dell U series monitors is more.

    • Conclusion

    The company Dell has manufactured monitors differently and according for the ease working of professionals in their respective fields. They can choose these monitors on the basis of their specifications and work profile. However, the overall performance of the Dell U Series monitors is outstanding and is quite popular in demand despite its high price. In the Dell U Series, the customer gets a brighter display, lightweight monitor, better native resolution, different ports, etc. On the other hand, if the customer is not specific about the display or brightness of its screen, they can choose the monitors of Dell P Series as they are best in quality with an affordable price range.
