Tragic Loss: Dax Tejera, Executive Producer of This Week, Dies Suddenly of A Heart Attack at 3

Publish date: 2024-05-23

With incredible sadness, we announce the sudden passing of Dax Tejera, executive producer of “This Week with George Stephanopoulos,” on ABC News. He passed away at age 37 on December 23 from a heart attack. 

In an emotional memo to staff, ABC News President Kim Godwin wrote with “a heavy heart and deep sadness”. She further described Dax as having an abundance of energy and passion for his work and deeply loving his daughters. 

His presence will be truly missed by everyone who was lucky enough to have known him.

Godwin expressed their deepest sympathies to the Tejera family during this difficult time. The loss of Tejera has been a deep sorrow for his wife Veronica and two young daughters especially. 

An outpouring of love and sympathy has been expressed by Tejera’s family, friends, and followers through words of condolences. They are also gathering at their home to comfort Veronica and her daughters in their grief. 

Though he was taken away too soon, his memories will remain intact in the hearts of his loved ones. It was made known that he passed away due to unforeseen circumstances after his untimely death. 

His life may have ended prematurely but he left behind priceless memories that will be cherished forever.

About Dax Tejera 

Tejera was a prolific Television producer who earned much fame during his lifetime.

In 2017, he began working with the American Broadcasting Company (ABC) and went on to produce remote-anchored broadcasts across the nation as well as coverage of important summits between former President Donald Trump and Vladimir Putin. 

With Tejera’s appointment as executive producer of This Week in 2020, the program surpassed all Sunday shows in its demographic ratings for adults 25–54. 

He distinguished himself from other producers by taking the show’s hosts out into the field, which diverted attention from the usual focus on Beltway politics.

His resume also includes newsmaker interviews and reporting on Covid, the 2020 presidential race, and even coverage of the attack on US Capitol Hill on January 6th.

He previously worked as a producer at MSNBC and HBO on the film Hate Rising.
