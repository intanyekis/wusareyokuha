Why is Hopkins called Nuk? Why do they call DeAndre Hopkins nuke?

Publish date: 2024-09-08

DeAndre Hopkins is an exceptionally gifted American football wide receiver for the Arizona Cardinals of the National Football League.

DeAndre Rashaun Hopkins was born on June 6, 1992, in Central, South Carolina, the United States of America to his parents Sabrina Greenlee, and Harris Steve Hopkins. He lost his father, Harris Steve Hopkins in a car accident on I-85 when he was only five months old.

DeAndre Hopkins was raised by his single mother. His mother Sabrina had worked at an automotive factory. He was raised alongside three siblings, two sisters Shanterria and Kesha as well as one brother Marcus. His two older siblings, Kesha Smith and Marcus Greenlee are from a previous relationship.

DeAndre Hopkins was an athletic student at D. W. Daniel High School in Central, South Carolina. He played football, and basketball, and ran track and field. In his senior season, he was named the Independent Mail’s player of the year and was rated as the 12th best wide receiver in the nation as well as the State of South Carolina.

DeAndre Hopkins earned an All-State first-team from the state and The Charlotte Observer. He attended Clemson University, where he played for the Clemson Tigers football team from 2010 to 2012. The Houston Texans selected DeAndre Hopkins in the first round with the 27th overall pick in the 2013 NFL Draft.

DeAndre Hopkins became the second wide receiver in franchise history to be drafted in the first round. He is a five-time Pro Bowler and has also been named to five All-Pro teams.

Why is Hopkins called Nuk?

DeAndre Hopkins is called Nuk, a nickname given to him by his mother because of his frequent chewing through NUK pacifiers.

Why do they call DeAndre Hopkins nuke?

DeAndre Hopkins was given the nickname Nuk by his mother. This was because he would frequently chew through NUK pacifiers.
