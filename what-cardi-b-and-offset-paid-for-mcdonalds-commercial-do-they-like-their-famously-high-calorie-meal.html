Cardi B And Offset's McDonald's Collaboration Sparked Backlash and Boycotts

McDonald's found itself caught in a storm of controversy surrounding its celebrity promotion, the Cardi B & Offset Meal. Some franchisees voiced concerns, alleging that the collaboration violated the internal guidelines known as the "Golden Arches Code." The franchisees argued that partnering with an entertainer known for explicit content could potentially harm the brand's family-friendly image.

The Cardi B & Offset Meal includes Cardi B's choice of a cheeseburger with BBQ sauce and a large Coca-Cola, along with Offset's preference for a Quarter Pounder with cheese and a large Hi-C Orange Lavaburst. The meal also features an apple pie and a large order of fries for sharing.

Numerous McDonald's restaurant owners expressed reservations about the partnership, citing that the pair's song lyrics and public image did not align with the brand's values. Some even refused to promote the meal altogether.

While it remains unclear how many franchises expressed concern or opted out of serving the meal, McDonald's stated that the promotion garnered "widespread support and excitement" from owners and employees. Defending their decision, McDonald's highlighted the long-standing tradition of Cardi B and Offset enjoying date nights at their restaurants.

Despite the controversy, McDonald's has a history of successful brand partnerships with artists like Travis Scott and Saweetie. The Travis Scott meal, introduced in 2020, led to a 4.6% increase in sales during a three-month period. However, a survey by the National Owners Association revealed that 65% of U.S. franchisees were not in favor of the partnership.

Interestingly, McDonald's attributed their prosperous third quarter of 2021, which witnessed a 9.6% rise in sales and a net income of $2.15 billion, to their collaboration with American rapper Saweetie.

McDonald's Responded To The Backlash Of The Cardi B & Offset Meal

In a whirlwind of flavor and controversy, McDonald's introduced its highly anticipated Cardi B and Offset promotion in a star-studded Super Bowl ad earlier this year. The commercial showcased the power couple, who have been happily married since 2017, indulging in a luxurious date while enjoying their go-to McDonald's order.

However, reports soon emerged of discontent among some McDonald's franchise owners. They expressed concerns that the couple's explicit lyrics and provocative content could tarnish the brand's family-friendly image.
